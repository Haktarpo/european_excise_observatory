Full methodology & sources

Every figure here is traceable to an official source line

This observatory shows tobacco, alcohol and energy excise duties as a share of each country's total taxation. It covers the EU-27 plus Iceland, Norway and Switzerland, for the years 2008 to 2024. Shares are ratios within a single country, so no currency conversion is involved.

Sources

Eurostat — National Tax List (NTL), 2025 edition
Excise revenues and total taxation, classified to ESA 2010. Unit: million national currency (euro-fixed for euro-area members). Last updated 22 April 2026.
ec.europa.eu — Tax revenue statistics
Office of the Revenue Commissioners (Ireland) — Excise Receipts by Commodity
Used only for Ireland's tobacco and alcohol, because the Eurostat list bundles "alcohol and tobacco" into single lines for Ireland and cannot be split. Energy and total tax for Ireland still come from Eurostat.

What each category contains

  • Total tax — Eurostat line ODB, "total receipts from taxes and social contributions" (the denominator; it includes compulsory social contributions, the standard EU definition).
  • Tobacco — excise lines named as tobacco, cigarette, cigar or nicotine products, including e-cigarette liquid where it is reported separately.
  • Alcohol — all alcohol/tobacco-classified excise minus tobacco: beer, wine, spirits, cider and intermediate products.
  • Energy — shown two ways. Excise only is fuel and electricity duty. Incl. carbon & ETS adds EU Emissions Trading auction revenue and national carbon levies, which Eurostat classifies as energy taxes.

Judgment calls (so nothing is over- or under-counted)

  • Only the most detailed (leaf) lines are summed, so subtotals are never double-counted.
  • Greece: two lines mixing coffee and e-cigarettes (€168m, 2024) are excluded from alcohol — they are neither.
  • Poland: a liquor-sale licence fee (€1,258m) is excluded from alcohol — it is a permit fee, not an excise.
  • France: all alcohol sits in one broad line, "Taxes on beverages." Eurostat tags it as alcohol/tobacco, but the label may include non-alcoholic or social-levy beverage taxes, and it cannot be split from this source. Treat France's alcohol figure as indicative.
  • Ireland tobacco shows a 2017 spike and 2018 dip — a real tax-stamp forestalling timing effect, not a data error.

Not yet included

Tax-to-GDP is not shown here. Adding it cleanly requires a matching GDP series (World Bank GDP in current local currency); once supplied it can be wired into the country profile without estimation.
